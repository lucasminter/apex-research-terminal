Asset Allocation Frameworks
Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT)Harry Markowitz · 1952
For any given level of expected return, there exists an optimal portfolio with minimum possible volatility — the "efficient frontier." Diversification reduces risk without sacrificing expected return, because assets don't move in perfect lockstep.
Ray Dalio's All Weather PortfolioBridgewater · Preset Available
Designed to perform across all four economic regimes. Each quadrant gets equal "risk contribution" rather than equal capital allocation.

Classic All Weather: 30% US Stocks · 40% Long-Term Bonds · 15% Intermediate Bonds · 7.5% Gold · 7.5% Commodities
The Macro Regime MatrixAPEX Framework
Markets cycle through four regimes defined by growth and inflation direction:

Q1 (↓ Growth / ↑ Inflation — Stagflation): Commodities, Energy, TIPS, Gold
Q2 (↓ Growth / ↓ Inflation — Inflationary Bust): TIPS, Energy, Short bonds
Q3 (↑ Growth / ↓ Inflation — Goldilocks): Equities, Credit, REITs
Q4 (↓ Growth / ↓ Inflation — Deflationary Bust): Long Treasuries, Cash, Gold
Key Metrics Explained
CAGR — Compound Annual Growth Rate
The rate at which a portfolio would have needed to grow each year to go from starting value to ending value. Does not reflect year-to-year volatility.
Sharpe RatioWilliam Sharpe · 1966
(Portfolio Return − Risk-Free Rate) ÷ Portfolio Volatility.

Benchmarks: >1.0 = Good · >1.5 = Very Good · >2.0 = Excellent · <0 = Worse than cash
Maximum Drawdown
Largest peak-to-trough decline before a new peak is achieved. The single most behaviorally relevant risk metric. Recovery math: A 50% drawdown requires a 100% gain to break even.
Monte Carlo Simulation
Runs hundreds of hypothetical future scenarios by randomly sampling monthly returns from a lognormal distribution calibrated to your portfolio's expected return and volatility. Use results as a range of outcomes, not a precise forecast.
